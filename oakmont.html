About Oakmont Country Club

Oakmont Country Club is one of the most iconic and challenging golf clubs in the United States, located just outside Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania. Founded in 1903 by Henry Clay Fownes, the course is renowned for its fast greens, deep bunkers, and minimal tree cover, creating a demanding test for even the world’s best golfers. Oakmont has hosted more major championships than almost any other course, including multiple U.S. Opens, solidifying its place in golf history. The club’s signature features, such as the famous “Church Pews” bunker, add to its distinct character and difficulty. Known for its tradition and commitment to preserving the original design, Oakmont Country Club remains a benchmark for championship golf.
